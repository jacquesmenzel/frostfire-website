Common HVAC Emergencies

No Cooling in Summer

When temps exceed 90¬∞F and your AC fails, heat exhaustion becomes a real danger ‚Äî especially for children, elderly, and pets. Don't wait. Call immediately.

No Heat in Winter

Frozen pipes, hypothermia risk, and property damage from extreme cold make a heating failure a true emergency. We prioritize no-heat calls in cold weather.

Gas Smell or CO Alarm

If you smell gas, evacuate immediately and call 911 first. Then call us. We'll inspect your furnace, gas lines, and heat exchanger for leaks or cracks.

Water Leak from Unit

AC condensate leaks can damage ceilings, walls, and floors. A clogged drain or frozen coil needs prompt attention to prevent costly water damage.

Burning Smell

Electrical burning odors from your HVAC system indicate overheating wires, failing motors, or electrical shorts. Turn off the system and call us right away.

Frozen AC Unit

Ice on your outdoor unit or evaporator coil signals airflow problems or refrigerant issues. Running a frozen system can destroy the compressor ‚Äî call for help.
